Honors Thesis
Implications of Construal Level and Risk Perceptions for Behavioral Intentions to Download Mobile Apps

My research at the University of Connecticut focused on Construal Level Theory of Psychological distance, which essentially holds that psychological distance can affect people's mental representations of targets. Specifically, as targets become more psychologically distance, they are mentally represented as more abstract, while psychologically closer objects are perceived in a more concrete way. I am interested in the implications for Construal Level on mobile applications.

Abstract

"We are interested in the factors that drive the adoption and use of mobile apps used to promote financial well being and personal health. We predicted that intentions would be related to personal perceptions of risk in these areas and that perceptions of risk can be affected by construal level. Specifically, we found in an online study that perceptions of health and finance risks increased with psychological distance from a target, except in the case of temporal distance. In turn, we found that perceptions of oneâ€™s own risks were positively related to behavioral intentions to download and use a mobile app. Additionally, we found perceptions of risk to be positively related to behavioral intentions across multiple dimensions of psychological distance. These findings suggest that construal level can have an effect on risk perceptions, which in turn are associated with behavioral intentions.""
